Rabbit Hole Detector

  1. Home
  2. Rabbit Hole Detector

Project information

  • Project date: 22 June 2025

As part of the Sundai hackathon, this project was developed to detect "rabbit holes" in online content, which are pathways that lead users deeper into a specific topic or ideology, often without presenting alternative viewpoints and therefore causing the user to be trapped in a "echo chamber". The tool aims to help users identify when they are being led down a biased or one-sided information path by monitoring their browser history and analyzing the content they consume. Due to the scope of the hackathon, the tool only analyses Firefox and Google search history and does not support other browsers.

My role in this project was to develop a data preprocessing pipeline that collects and processes the user's browser history data, extracting relevant information such as URLs, timestamps, and webpage content, so that information can be extracted by a text embedding model.
